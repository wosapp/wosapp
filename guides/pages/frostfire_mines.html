Frostfire Mines

Learn how to micro your marches during Frostfire Mines event and climb the leaderboard with a very simple strategy

Event Details
  • When: the event occurs on Tuesdays every two weeks
  • Participation: players can sign up during the event day (sign up closes 10 minutes before the fight starts). If you sign up and miss one Frostfire Mines fight you can sign up for another time slot.
  • Time schedule:
    • 02:00 UTC
    • 05:00 UTC
    • 11:00 UTC
    • 14:00 UTC
    • 16:00 UTC
    • 18:00 UTC
    • 21:00 UTC
  • Duration: 30 minutes
  • Goal: collect as much Orichalcum as possible from the different sources
  • Rewards: players receive rewards based on the total points accumulated and their ranking in the leaderboard. Rewards consist in Chief Charm material (up to 80 Charm Design and 80 Charm Guides), as well as gems, speed ups and resources
How to collect Orichalcum points There are three main ways to collect Orichalcum points during the Frostfire Mines event:
  • Orichalcum Veins: these mines are scattered around the map and can be captured by players. Each mine provides a steady stream of Orichalcum points while it is under your control. Level 1, 2 and 3 veins are available and provide 8, 16 and 32 Orichalcum points every second of occupation (up to 8,640, 17,280 and 34,560 respectively). The map is divided in three sections, with the level 3 veins located in the third level around the center of the map.
  • Veins Outburst: these are temporary locations that spawn during the event. Players can gather Orichalcum points from these deposits, base gathering rate is 200/s with a maximum of 4,000.
  • Smelter occupation time: located at the center of the map, the Smelter gives points based on the total occupation time ranking:
    • 1: 100,000 points
    • 2: 80,000 points
    • 3: 60,000 points
    • 4: 55,000 points
    • 5: 50,000 points
    • 6: 40,000 points
    • 7: 35,000 points
    • 8: 30,000 points
    • 9: 25,000 points
    • 10: 20,000 points

Skill points can be earned by defeating Patrol Guards. Level 1 Patrol Guards give 75 XP points when defeated, while level 2 guards give 150 XP points.

Strategy and gameplay

Most of the damage is dealt by Marksmen and Lancers, while Infantry contributes marginally. Preset your marches accordingly, prioritizing Marksmen, followed by Lancers and finally Infantry.

In orderd to prepare your marches in advance, calculate the maximum deployable troops for your marches:

  • If your total troops do not exceed the maximum deployable troops you can simply equalize all your marches
  • If your troops exceed the maximum deployable troops: split Marksmen and Lancers among the available marches, then fill the rest with infantry. Note that if you lead a rally you have an extra march available. For example if you can deploy 6 marches and you are a rally starter, your total marches is 7.
  • If you are a strong rally leader, consider setting your rally with 1 infantry, 20% lancers and 80% marksmen (only if you have at least 25-30% of marksmen in the other marches to join other players rallies)
